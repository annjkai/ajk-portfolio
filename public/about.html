About me

I do all my own coding stunts

After many years in the language services industry, I decided in 2016 that it was time for a career change. After a brief foray into Android, I fell in love with JavaScript and front-end dev in 2017.

In late 2017, I enrolled in a front-end web developer scholarship, sponsored by Google, and was one of 10% of students selected from 20,00 to receive a full Nanodegree scholarship. I am proud to say that I attained my Front-End Web Developer Nanodegree on August 16, 2018.

Experience

I hold a BA (Hons.) in Linguistics from the University of Calgary, AB, Canada. In 2012 I relocated to Leipzig to work as a Business English Trainer, which very naturally led into a career as a translator, proofreader and text editor. While this allowed me to deepen my skills in and love for iterative, independent work which delivers an incredible product, I found myself often frustrated at this relatively technology-averse industry. I then spent several years in project management roles, during which I also decided to invest in my tech skills by learning to code.

While learning programming as a novice is no joke, I realized quite quickly that I loved pushing around pixels and making things on my screen do stuff. Thanks to the rigorous, fast-paced curriculum of the Udacity Nanodegree, I've built up a strong repertoire of skills in most of the fundamental front-end web technologies.
